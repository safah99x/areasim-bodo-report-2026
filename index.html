11 What this all means

The four findings
that actually matter.

Numbers earn their keep when they shape decisions. Here are the four that should shape yours.

You're over-seated by three times.
3.7×

You have 3.7 seats per employee. The global benchmark is 1.0 to 1.2. You could halve your seating tomorrow and still comfortably accommodate the busiest day we observed.

Most of your rent pays for empty chairs.
79%

On average, nearly four out of every five seats sit vacant. If your office costs X per month, roughly 0.79X is going to space that no one is using. Every month. Without exception.

Floor 3 is your sweet spot.
62%

Floor 3 leads at 62% utilization. Floor 6 trails at 18%. Consolidating activity to three or four floors could meaningfully cut operating costs, with zero impact on productivity.

Fridays are quiet. Very quiet.
~40%

Friday attendance drops to around 40% of a normal weekday. You now have the evidence to formalize flexible work policies and, perhaps, negotiate reduced operating costs for the quietest days.

12 Where to start

Three moves
to make this quarter.

Plenty more could follow, but these three are the highest-leverage places to begin.

01

Right-size your seating.

With peak attendance of 227 and a generous 1.2× buffer, you need around 272 seats, not 1,275. A reduction of roughly 78% of your seating footprint is achievable while still comfortably handling your busiest days. Free up the floor space for flexible zones, focus rooms, or simply, less rent.

02

Consolidate to your active floors.

Floors 1, 2, and 3 carry most of the activity. Decommission or repurpose floors 5 and 6, which together average under 25% use. Switching off HVAC, lighting, and cleaning on two full floors delivers measurable savings within weeks, not months.

03

Re-count in six months.

Office behaviour shifts, particularly as hybrid policies evolve. A second study in six months will confirm these trends, measure the impact of whatever changes you make, and give you the longitudinal data that boards quietly require for any real estate decision.
